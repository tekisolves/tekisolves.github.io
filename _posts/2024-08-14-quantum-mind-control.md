## Understanding Quantum Mechanics and the Observer Effect

In quantum mechanics, the observer effect is a well-known phenomenon where the act of observation alters the state of a quantum system. Particles can exist in multiple states at once—a superposition—until we observe them, forcing them into a single state. This raises an intriguing question: if our observations can shape reality at the quantum level, could they also influence quantum computers?
