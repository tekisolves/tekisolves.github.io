## Decoding Financial Terms: Your Essential Finance Glossary
<img src="/tekibg.png" alt="Teki Solves Home BG" title="Teki Solves Home BG" class="img-fluid">

To truly understand your financial statements, it’s important to know the key terms that make up these reports. Here’s a simple glossary of the most important financial terms every entrepreneur should know:

**1. Assets**
Everything your business owns that has value, such as cash, equipment, or property.

**2. Liabilities**
What your business owes, such as loans, bills, or other debts.

**3. Equity**
The ownership value in your business. It’s the difference between your assets and liabilities.

**4. Revenue**
The income your business generates from sales or services.

**5. Expenses**
The costs of running your business, including salaries, rent, and utilities.

**6. Net Income**
The profit after all expenses have been deducted from revenue.

**7. Cash Flow**
The movement of money in and out of your business, showing whether you have enough liquidity to meet your obligations.

**8. Gross Profit**
Revenue minus the cost of goods sold (COGS), showing how much you earn from core activities.

**9. Operating Income**
The profit from your business operations, before taxes and interest.

**10. Current Ratio**
A measure of a business’s ability to cover its short-term liabilities with its short-term assets.

**11. Return on Investment (ROI)**
A measure of the profitability of an investment relative to its cost.

## Conclusion
Understanding these basic financial terms is a crucial step in improving your business knowledge. Keep them in mind as you dive deeper into financial statements!
